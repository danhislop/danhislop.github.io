Music

Mostly I just love to listen to music. It vibes my workday, fills my weekends, and transforms mere car rides into road trips.

But music is so much more. It's a creative outlet like no other, packing feelings into sprawling containers, just waiting to release the emotions in our bodies. It's some kind of native language whose magic works whether I understand it or not. Below are some notes on how I've connected with music so far.

Watercolor of my favorite guitar, a Taylor 810 WMB

Big Empty House

I’ve made just enough music to appreciate how hard it is to do well. A student of songwriting, over the years I’d collected just enough songs for an album, and recorded a full length album in 2006 called Big Empty House. It was so fun - gathering musical friends to play, working with a killer producer, choosing the CD artwork and layout, even a CD release party : ) See the original website here.

I learned a lot from the process, about which styles work “for me”, where in the musical landscape I work best (writing, collaborating) and where to outsource (singing or solos). I love looking back at the risk I took to make this CD and that it exists as a sort of “photo album” for that time in my life.

Live Music

This photo shown is from one of my top 5 all-time concerts - Little Simz at the Novo. Big venue shows are great too!
